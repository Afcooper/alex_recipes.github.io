Brunch · Serves 2–3

Weekend Shakshuka

Eggs gently poached in a spiced tomato and pepper sauce. The kind of thing you make once and then make every Sunday. Eat it straight from the pan with bread for scooping.

  • Prep 10 min
  • Cook 25 min
  • Serves 2–3
  • One pan

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely sliced
  • 1 red pepper, sliced
  • 3 garlic cloves, sliced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp sweet paprika
  • ¼ tsp chilli flakes
  • 1 × 400g tin chopped tomatoes
  • 4 eggs
  • to serve feta, parsley, crusty bread

Method

  1. Warm the oil in a wide frying pan over medium heat. Add the onion and pepper with a pinch of salt and cook gently for 8–10 minutes until soft and sweet.
  2. Stir in the garlic, cumin, paprika and chilli flakes. Cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Pour in the tomatoes, half-fill the tin with water and add that too. Simmer for 10 minutes until thickened, seasoning to taste.
  4. Make four wells in the sauce and crack an egg into each. Cover and cook 5–7 minutes, until the whites are set but the yolks still wobble.
  5. Scatter over crumbled feta and parsley. Serve straight from the pan with bread.

Make it yours: a spoonful of harissa stirred into the sauce takes it somewhere smokier; a handful of spinach wilted in before the eggs makes it greener.

Dinner · Serves 2

Proper Carbonara

The whole trick is heat control: the sauce is cooked by the residual warmth of the pasta, not the flame. Get that right and you get silk, not scrambled eggs. No cream goes anywhere near it.

  • Prep 5 min
  • Cook 15 min
  • Serves 2
  • No cream

Ingredients

  • 200g spaghetti or rigatoni
  • 100g guanciale (or pancetta), diced
  • 3 egg yolks + 1 whole egg
  • 50g pecorino romano, finely grated
  • plenty black pepper, freshly ground
  • to taste salt (for the pasta water)

Method

  1. Bring a large pot of well-salted water to the boil and cook the pasta until just shy of al dente.
  2. Meanwhile, fry the guanciale in a cold, dry pan brought up to medium heat until the fat renders and it crisps. Take off the heat.
  3. Whisk the yolks, whole egg, pecorino and a heavy grind of pepper into a thick paste.
  4. Reserve a mug of pasta water, then drain. Toss the hot pasta into the guanciale pan (off the heat).
  5. Add a splash of pasta water to the egg mix to loosen it, then pour over the pasta, tossing hard. Add more water a little at a time until the sauce coats every strand glossily.
  6. Serve at once with extra pecorino and pepper.

The save: if the pan is too hot when the egg goes in, it scrambles. Always off the heat — the pasta carries enough warmth to cook the sauce through.

Vegetarian · Serves 4

Tarka Dal

Humble, cheap and deeply comforting. The lentils do their quiet thing while you make the tarka — the sizzling spiced oil poured over at the end that makes the whole dish sing.

  • Prep 10 min
  • Cook 30 min
  • Serves 4
  • Freezer friendly

Ingredients

  • 250g red lentils, rinsed
  • 1 litre water
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • — For the tarka:
  • 3 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 4 garlic cloves, sliced
  • 1 dried red chilli
  • pinch asafoetida (optional)

Method

  1. Put the lentils, water and turmeric in a pot. Bring to the boil, skim off any froth, then simmer partly covered for 20–25 minutes, stirring now and then, until soft and porridge-like.
  2. Stir in the chopped tomato and season generously with salt. Loosen with a little hot water if it’s too thick.
  3. For the tarka, heat the ghee in a small pan until shimmering. Add the cumin seeds and let them crackle for a few seconds.
  4. Add the garlic, dried chilli and asafoetida. Fry until the garlic turns pale gold — watch it, it burns fast.
  5. Pour the sizzling tarka over the dal (it will splutter). Stir half through, leave half on top. Serve with rice or flatbread.

Tip: the dal thickens as it sits, so keep that mug of hot water handy to bring it back to a spoonable consistency before serving.

Bake · Makes 12

Brown Butter Cookies

Browning the butter is the only extra step, and it’s the whole point: it gives these a toffee-ish, almost nutty depth. Crisp at the edge, chewy in the middle, better the next day.

  • Prep 20 min
  • Chill 30 min
  • Bake 11 min
  • Makes 12

Ingredients

  • 170g unsalted butter
  • 150g light brown sugar
  • 75g caster sugar
  • 1 egg + 1 yolk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 220g plain flour
  • ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • ½ tsp fine salt
  • 200g dark chocolate, chopped
  • to finish flaky sea salt

Method

  1. Melt the butter in a pan over medium heat, swirling, until it foams, smells nutty and the milk solids turn golden-brown. Pour into a bowl (scrape in the brown bits) and cool 10 minutes.
  2. Whisk both sugars into the warm butter, then the egg, yolk and vanilla, until glossy and smooth.
  3. Fold in the flour, bicarb and salt until just combined, then fold through the chopped chocolate. Chill the dough for 30 minutes.
  4. Heat the oven to 180°C (fan 160°C). Roll the dough into 12 balls and space well apart on lined trays.
  5. Bake for 10–12 minutes until the edges are set but the centres look slightly underdone. Finish with flaky salt and cool on the tray for 5 minutes to firm up.

For neat rounds: the second the cookies come out, swirl a round cookie cutter or glass around each one on the tray to nudge the edges into a perfect circle.
