A quick five-ingredient berry sauce that turns jammy as it cools — perfect over pancakes, yogurt, French toast, or ice cream.

## Ingredients
- 2 cups fresh or frozen blueberries
- 1/4 cup maple syrup
- 2 tbsp water
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

## Steps
1. **Simmer the base:** Add half the blueberries (about 1 cup) to a small saucepan with the {{maple syrup}}, {{water}}, {{fresh lemon juice}}, and {{vanilla extract}}. Bring to a simmer over medium heat and cook 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. @timer 10m
2. **Add the rest of the berries:** Stir in the remaining blueberries and cook another 8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the berries burst and the sauce looks glossy. @timer 8m
3. **Cool to thicken:** Pull it off the heat. The compote keeps thickening into a jammy sauce as it cools, so give it a few minutes before serving. @timer 5m

## Notes
Keeps in the fridge up to 5 days and freezes well — try freezing it in an ice cube tray for single servings to drop on oatmeal or pancakes later.

For a thicker, brighter sauce, whisk in a pinch of cornstarch partway through and add a little lemon zest in the last few minutes.
