Thick challah or brioche soaked in vanilla-cinnamon custard and cooked low and slow in butter — crisp edges, custardy center.

## Ingredients
- 1 |  | loaf challah or brioche bread, thick-sliced
- 2 cups half-and-half or heavy cream
- 4 large eggs
- 1/4 cup sugar
- 2 tsp vanilla extract
- 1/2 tsp cinnamon
- 3 tbsp salted butter, for the pan (plus more as needed)

## Steps
1. **Slice the bread:** Cut the {{loaf challah or brioche bread}} into ~1-inch slices. Day-old or slightly stale is best — sturdier, and soaks up more custard without falling apart.
2. **Make the custard:** Whisk the {{half-and-half or heavy cream}}, {{large eggs}}, {{sugar}}, {{vanilla extract}}, and {{cinnamon}} until combined. For the smoothest custard with no streaks, blitz it with an immersion blender so the cinnamon fully blends in.
3. **Soak the slices:** Dip each slice, soaking about 20 seconds per side, then let the excess drip off. @timer 40s
4. **Cook low and slow:** Melt a generous amount of {{salted butter}} in a skillet over medium-low heat. Cook each slice 3–5 minutes per side until deeply golden — keep it low and slow so the custardy center cooks through before the outside over-browns. Drop to low if it's coloring too fast. @timer 5m
5. **Check and serve:** Press the center gently: firm means done, jiggly means it needs longer. Serve right away with powdered sugar, a pat of butter, and real maple syrup.

## Notes
Cooking in batches? Hold finished slices in a 180°F oven so everything stays warm and crisp. Pairs perfectly with the blueberry compote above.

The bread is the star — sturdy challah or brioche makes all the difference.
