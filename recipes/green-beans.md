Blistered, smoky green beans cooked hard and fast in a screaming-hot cast iron pan.

## Ingredients
- 1 lb green beans
- 1 tbsp avocado oil (or olive oil)
- 1 tsp paprika
- 1 tsp onion powder
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper

## Steps
1. **Get the pan ripping hot:** Set a cast iron skillet over high heat and let it get properly hot, about 3–4 minutes. Add the {{avocado oil}} and give it a few seconds to heat up. @timer 4m
2. **Char the beans:** Add the {{green beans}} in a single layer — make sure they're trimmed and patted dry so they char instead of steam. Cook 10–15 minutes, stirring only occasionally, until blistered and charred to your liking. @timer 12m
3. **Season at the end:** In the last 3–5 minutes, sprinkle in the {{paprika}}, {{onion powder}}, {{garlic powder}}, {{salt}}, and {{black pepper}}. Toss to coat and let the spices toast against the hot beans, then pull from the heat. @timer 4m

## Notes
Dry beans are non-negotiable — any surface water steams them and kills the char.

Avocado oil is the move here because it takes high heat without burning, unlike butter or olive oil. Want extra richness? Toss with a small knob of butter off the heat at the very end.
