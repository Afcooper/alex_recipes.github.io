Crisp-skinned pan-seared salmon fillets, optionally finished with butter or soy garlic sauce.

## Ingredients
- salmon, thick center cut
- canola spray
- salt

## Steps
1. **Bring to temp:** Remove the salmon from the fridge and let stand at room temperature for 10 minutes. With a paper towel, pat the fillets very dry on both sides. @timer 10m
2. **Heat the pan:** Heat a skillet over medium heat, about 3-5 minutes. It's important the pan is hot before you add the salmon, or it won't crisp properly.
3. **Season:** Just before adding salmon to the pan, spray the pan with canola oil spray and season the flesh side of the salmon with 1/2 teaspoon salt.
4. **Sear first side:** Place the fillets in the skillet, skin-side up. Press down firmly with a spatula to ensure good browning. Let the salmon cook on the first side on medium to medium-low, undisturbed, until the flesh appears cooked about 3/4 of the way up the fillet, about 3 to 5 minutes (depends on thickness).
5. **Finish:** Reduce the pan heat. Cook the salmon on the other side until it is done to your liking, about 30 seconds. Remove from pan and use a spatula to remove the skin. Add 1 tbsp butter (optional) or soy garlic sauce (see recipe).
