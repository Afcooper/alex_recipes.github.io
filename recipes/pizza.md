King Arthur '00' Neapolitan dough topped with a quick seasoned tomato sauce, mozzarella and parmesan, Italian sausage, and a finishing drizzle of hot honey. Makes two 10–12" pies.

## Ingredients
- 2 cups King Arthur '00' pizza flour
- 1/8 tsp instant or active dry yeast
- 1/2 tsp granulated sugar
- 1 1/4 tsp table salt
- 3/4 cup lukewarm water (105–115°F)
- 1 cup tomato sauce
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp onion powder
- 1/4 tsp salt (for the sauce)
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp Italian seasoning
- 2 cups shredded mozzarella
- 1/2 cup grated parmesan
- 1/2 lb Italian sausage, casings removed
- 2 tbsp hot honey, for drizzling

## Steps
1. **Mix the dough (night before):** The night before, whisk the {{King Arthur '00' pizza flour}}, {{instant or active dry yeast}}, {{granulated sugar}}, and {{table salt}} in a medium bowl. Add the {{lukewarm water}} and stir until it comes together into a rough, shaggy dough.
2. **Rise overnight (12–24 hrs):** Cover the bowl and let it rise at room temperature at least 12 hours and up to 24. It won't double — it'll just get a bit puffy. @timer 12h
3. **Preheat the oven + stone:** When you're ready to bake, set a baking steel or stone on a center rack and preheat the oven as hot as it goes (500–550°F) for at least 30 minutes. A fully preheated stone is what gives you a crisp bottom. @timer 30m
4. **Divide and shape into balls:** Divide the dough in half (about 200g each). On a well-floured surface, stretch-and-fold each piece: grab both ends, pull apart, fold back over itself, and repeat on all four sides. Then tuck the edges under into a smooth ball, seam-side down.
5. **Second rise (45–60 min):** Set each ball seam-side down in a floured bowl, cover, and let rise 45–60 minutes while the oven heats. @timer 45m
6. **Make the sauce:** Stir together the {{tomato sauce}}, {{garlic powder}}, {{onion powder}}, {{salt}}, {{black pepper}}, and {{Italian seasoning}}. That's your pizza sauce — taste and adjust.
7. **Prep the sausage:** Pinch the {{Italian sausage}} into small, thin pieces (about ½ inch) so they cook through in the short, hot bake — or brown it in a skillet first if you'd rather be sure.
8. **Shape the crust:** Move one ball to a floured surface, seam-side down. Press the center with your fingertips, leaving the outer rim untouched (that's what puffs into the crust). Using your knuckles and gravity, stretch it into a 10–12" round — if it fights you, rest it 5–10 minutes and try again. Transfer to a well-floured peel or a sheet of parchment.
9. **Sauce and top:** Spread a thin layer of sauce over the crust, leaving a border. Scatter on the {{shredded mozzarella}} and {{grated parmesan}}, then top with the sausage pieces.
10. **Bake hot and fast:** Slide the pizza onto the hot stone and bake 6–8 minutes, until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbling. For extra char, finish under the top broiler 2–3 minutes — but only with at least 8" between the stone and the broiler, and watch it closely. @timer 8m
11. **Finish with hot honey:** Pull it out and immediately drizzle with {{hot honey}}. Cool a minute, then slice and serve. Reshape and top the second ball while the stone comes back up to temp, and repeat.

## Notes
Makes two 10–12" pizzas — this is King Arthur's '00' dough straight off the bag, with your sauce, sausage, and hot-honey build. For best results, weigh the dough: 232g flour and 170g water.

Short on time? Use the bag's faster method: bump the yeast to ½ tsp, rest the mixed dough 20–30 minutes (with one round of stretch-and-folds), then rise at room temperature about 8 hours instead of overnight.

Salt note: 1¼ tsp in 2 cups of flour runs a touch salty — dial it back toward 1 tsp if you prefer. No pizza stone? A preheated upside-down baking sheet or cast iron skillet works; just give it the full 30-minute preheat. Hot honey: store-bought is easy, or warm honey with a pinch of chili flakes and let it steep.
