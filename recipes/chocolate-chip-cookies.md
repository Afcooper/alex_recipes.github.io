Brown-butter chocolate chip cookies with a chewy, nutty toffee flavor.

## Ingredients
- 1 3/4 cups flour
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp baking soda
- 2 tsp vanilla extract
- 14 tbsp unsalted butter
- 1 large egg + 1 large egg yolk
- 1/2 cup white sugar
- 1 3/4 cups semisweet chocolate chips
- 3/4 cup dark brown sugar (packed)

## Steps
1. Whisk flour and baking soda together, set aside.
2. Heat 10 tbsp butter in a skillet (not a nonstick) over medium-high heat until melted. Continue cooking, swirling the pan constantly, until butter is dark golden brown and has a nutty aroma.
3. In a large heatproof bowl add 4 tbsp butter. Transfer the melted butter to this bowl and mix until all is melted.
4. Add both sugars, salt, and vanilla to the bowl with butter and whisk until fully incorporated. Add egg and yolk and whisk until smooth with no lumps. Let mixture stand 3 minutes, then whisk for 30 seconds; repeat the process 2 more times until the mix is thick, smooth and shiny. @timer 3m
5. Stir in flour mixture until combined.
6. Stir in chocolate chips.
7. Set covered mixture in the refrigerator.
8. Scoop into balls with a flat bottom. Place on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
9. Bake 8-12 minutes in a 375F oven. Or place in the freezer for 45 minutes, then place in a freezer-safe baggie and back in the freezer to enjoy whenever! @timer 12m

## Notes
Freeze unbaked dough balls for 45 minutes, then store in a freezer-safe baggie to bake whenever.
